# PlaceMark — Community Impact Toolkit

> A participatory evaluation toolkit for community-led creative placemaking organisations.  
> Built as a KTP prototype in the context of a proposed Knowledge Transfer Partnership between **The Stove Network** (Dumfries, Scotland) and the **University of Strathclyde**.

---

## 🌿 What is PlaceMark?

PlaceMark is a web-based evaluation toolkit that helps community arts and creative placemaking organisations measure, understand, and communicate the social, economic, cultural, and civic value of their work — without needing a research background.

It addresses a well-documented gap in the cultural sector: the absence of robust, accessible, and participatory frameworks capable of evidencing the full impact of community-based arts activity to commissioners, funders, and policymakers.

This prototype was developed to demonstrate the concept and technical feasibility of the proposed toolkit as part of a KTP application to Innovate UK/UKRI. It illustrates what a fully developed software version of the toolkit could look like, and forms part of a KTP research proposal focused on co-designing a nationally significant participatory evaluation framework for Scotland's community arts sector.

## 🗂️ The Five Modules

| Module | What it does |
|--------|-------------|
| **① Project Setup** | Enter project details and build your Theory of Change interactively |
| **② Indicator Selection** | Choose from 14 indicators across five value domains — social, cultural, economic, place, and civic |
| **③ Data Collection** | Access ready-to-use surveys, interview guides, focus group tools, and Most Significant Change forms |
| **④ Impact Dashboard** | Visualise your data in real time — charts, statistics, and community quotes |
| **⑤ Report Generator** | Generate formatted impact reports tailored for funders, policymakers, or communities |

---

## 📊 The Five Value Domains

The toolkit measures impact across five domains, combining quantitative and qualitative evidence:

- **Social** — belonging, isolation, new relationships
- **Cultural** — local pride, heritage documentation, creative participation
- **Economic** — SROI, local spend, employment
- **Place** — safety perception, activation of neglected spaces
- **Civic** — community agency, participation in decision-making

---

## 🔬 Research Context

This prototype sits within a broader academic research proposal. The underpinning research questions are:

> *How can a participatory evaluation framework be designed and validated to effectively evidence the social, economic, and cultural value of community-led creative placemaking for diverse stakeholder audiences?*

The methodology draws on:
- Theory of Change modelling
- Participatory Action Research (PAR)
- Mixed-method evaluation (quantitative surveys + qualitative narrative inquiry)
- Most Significant Change (MSC) technique
- Social Return on Investment (SROI) analysis
- Thematic analysis and triangulation

The proposed KTP would run over 24 months, testing and iterating the framework across live project partnerships including **Creative Stranraer** and **LIFT D&G**, with the goal of producing a toolkit suitable for sector-wide adoption across Scotland and beyond.

## 🗺️ Development Roadmap

If the KTP is awarded, the toolkit will be delivered across 24 months in the following phases:

| Phase | Timeline | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------|
| **Phase 1** | Months 1–10 | Literature review, co-design workshops, framework development |
| **Phase 2** | Months 11–18 | Build working prototype (Modules 1–3) with real database backend |
| **Phase 3** | Months 19–24 | Dashboard, report generator, live testing with partner organisations |
| **Phase 4** | Post-KTP | Full stack deployment, sector-wide adoption, commercial consultancy offer |

**Planned full-stack architecture:**

- **Frontend** — React.js
- **Backend** — Python (Django or Flask)
- **Database** — PostgreSQL
- **Data Visualisation** — Plotly / Chart.js
- **Report Generation** — python-docx / PDF libraries
- **Hosting** — AWS or Heroku
